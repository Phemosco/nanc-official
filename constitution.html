NANC ANATOMY

INTRODUCTION OF NANC

National Association of Nigerian Comrade (NANC) was founded in the year 2010 June 2nd by Comrade Ogunolu Samsideen Opeyemi a.k.a peace maker then he was a final year student, he founded NANC for the benefit of entire Nigerians by bringing together human right activist and social crusaders to fight in one accord for the betterment of our Nationsâ€™. Also to promote the interest of Nigerian Youths and Nigerian Students for the benefit of our generation.

NANC VISION

To unite activists in Nigeria towards a common cause, we believe that with proper leadership, discipline of truth & defender of democracy we can fight in one accord.

NANC MISSION

The NATIONAL ASSOCIATION OF NIGERIAN COMRADES is a child of necessity on a mission to bring together activists, social crusaders, progressives & liberals 4 united struggle for the total liberation of Nigeria and our people. We believe that only through solidarity can this historic battle be won. We are unreservedly devoted to truth, accountability, justice and democracy in Nigeria. For us, tribe and religion inconsequential we fight in defense of our conscience and posterity. We will never submit to tyranny or oppression, fear and persecution in the course of this struggle..

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

  1. To Embark on Youth Empowerment Initiatives via Award of Scholarships, Job placements and Skills acquisition.
  2. To provide Counseling to Nigerian Youths on Challenges in Academics, Career, Relationship etc., and General Life Issues like Rape, Addiction, Sexual Harassment, among others.
  3. To Organize Seminars, Symposiums, Talk Shows and lectures through viable media with a view to Educating the Youths about the dangers of societal Ills.
  4. To organize Rallies and to Employ and Utilize the Social/Mass Media in the Promotion of the Image of Nigeria all around the world, Campaign against Corrupt Practices In the System, bring Pressing Rural Communities and Grass Root Issues to the Forefront and Present them before the Government for Consideration, while we Consistently remind all Elected Public Office Holders those promises they made during their Campaigns and Manifestos.
  5. To generally and always Promote Peace, Love, Unity and Patriotism thereby Discouraging Tribalism, Ethnicity, Nepotism and Corruption.

Join This Train:

  • To Promote Unity amongst Nigerians from different background, culture, ethnic group and religion. To Encourage Patriotism amidst Nigerians home and abroad.
  • To Encourage Nigerians to Promote the kind of Nigeria they deserve by Projecting and saying it.
  • To promote a positive change in our mindset about the way we talk about our beloved country wherever we go.



CHAPTERS

CHAPTER 1

NAME

The association shall be known as the National Association of Nigerian Comrades And hereafter in this constitution shall be referred to as the "Association".

CHAPTER 2

BYE-LAW

The use of the name, initials, and emblem of the association shall be restricted to properly affiliated and qualified members of the association and shall not be used by others without the written consent of the president.

CHAPTER 3

AFFILIATION

The association shall be affiliated to the Nigerian Labour Congress NLC and the Joint Action Front JAF

CHAPTER 5

MEMBERSHIP:

ARTICLE 5-1

ANY person who is proposed as a member of the Association shall have his application form presented by a proposer and a seconder who shall both be bonafide members of the Association i.e. having paid all dues; whereupon a sanction of the Association at a general meeting and payment of all dues and membership fee and evidence of three consecutive observations, such proposed member shall be recognized as a member of the Association.

MEMBERâ€™S RULES AND REGULATION
  1. In every meeting, a sum of an amount will be collected from every member of the minutes.
  2. Punctuality is the soul of every business, whom so ever foul to be punctual in meeting will not be counted as a serious member in this Association and such a person will not have access to some benefits of the Association.
  3. When coming to the meeting, all members must be well dressed in order to look great and dynamic, and any member who violates this rule will pay a fine.
  4. Smoking and alcohol is not allowed during the meeting.
  5. The president even secretary has the right to call in an emergency meeting when necessary.
  6. When the meeting is in progress, no member should go out without the permission from the executives and all phones must be on vibration in order not to distract the attention of other member. Any member found guilty will pay a fine.
  7. The slogan of the Association must be raise before the approaching and repose must be replied by other member, anybody that fail will pay fine.
  8. No abusive word or slogan at the meeting.
  9. The association agenda should not be discussed outside the meeting or on social media.

ARTICLE 5-2:

QUALIFICATION

Membership of the association shall be open to all Individuals in Nigeria provided that such Individuals:
Comply with the requirements of the association as laid down in this constitution Have objectives which are consistent with the purpose of the association.
Maintain age limit of not less than 18years and more than 40years provided that membership may continue.

CONFIRMATION OF MEMBERSHIP
The president shall be the one to confirm any new or incoming member after he or she meet up with the requirement, and accept to participate 2/3 of NANC functions at a particular scheduled through his/her competency before he/she can be finally welcomed as a bonafide member of NANC at the convention.

ARTICLE 5-3:

RESIGNATION

A member may terminate his association with the association and may cease to be associated immediately upon his lodging of his wish to do so with the Secretary-General accompanied by all moneys and other property due to the association.

ARTICLE 5-4:

DISQULIFICATION

Where member of the council are of the opinion that a member is no longer qualified for membership of the association, the association may suspend or terminate the membership of the individual through a two-third vote of the council.
Expulsion will take place if there is contumacy or failure to comply or abide with the rules and regulations of organization constitutionally.

CHAPTER 6

ARTICLE 6-1

SUPREME AUTHORITY

The General Assembly shall constitute the supreme authority of the Association. It shall direct the affairs of the Association and shall retain all rights and privileges not specifically assigned to the Board of Directors, the President or any other council members.

BYE-LAW 6-2

EXCLUSIVE PREROGATIVE:

The General Assembly shall have the exclusive prerogative to vote on any and all of the following matters:

  • Election of all elective officers Approval of the list appointed officers.
  • Approval of the association's budget and programs of the ensuring year determine the Association's convention venue and venues for other occasions Amendments
  • Approval of the association constitution and long range plain Approve committees set up by the president.

    • BYE-LAW 6-3:

    SPECIFIC RESPONSIBILITIES

    The General Assembly shall receive and act upon reports recommendations referred to it by the Board of Directors or any member of the body.

    ARTICLE 6-4:

    COMPOSITION

    The General Assembly shall be composed of all member of the Association.

    BYE LAW 6-5:

    If the President is unable to attend a meeting of the General Assembly, the first or second vice-presidents should preside over the meeting in the above order.

    ARTICLE 6-6:

    VOTING

    In the vote by the general assembly, all members whose current dues and levies as prescribed by the constitution are paid shall have a franchise and are eligible to vote for any respective office.
    The sitting executives should be dissolved Three (3) weeks before the elections and a caretaker committee takes over to oversee the electoral process.

    WHO ARE ELIGIBLE TO VOTE?
    • Recognized Members are eligible to vote for state coordinators
    • States coordinators are eligible to vote for VPs
    • State coordinators and VPs are eligible to vote for president.

    ARTICLE 6-7:

    PROXIES

    Proxies shall not be recognized

    ARTICLE 6-8:

    CASTING OF VOTE

    The sitting President shall cast a vote in any election and also in the case of a tie in the election of the council members and cases where two-third majority are required.

    ARTICLE 6-9:

    QUORUM

    A quorum for the General Assembly shall comprise members having two-third of the total votes. For monthly business meeting of the General Assembly, ten members shall be recognized as a quorum.

    ARTICLE 6-10:

    The monthly meeting of the general assembly shall be held any third Sunday of the month or any other Sunday as deemed convenient by the House. However, special meeting may be called by a majority vote of the House.

    CHAPTER 10

    BOARD OF DIRECTORS

    ARTICLE 10-1:

    The elected and appointed officers of the ASSOCIATION plus the chairmen of the various committees should make up the Governing Body of the ASSOCIATION.
    During the term of office, the Board of Directors shall have control of the property of the ASSOCIATION including the administration of funds.
    It shall discharge all functions of the ASSOCIATION save those exercised by the General Assembly.

    BYE-LAW 10-2:
    SPECIFIC DUTIES
    The specific duties of the Board of Directors shall be:
    1. To train and brief all officers on their duties and responsibilities
    2. To co-ordinate the activities of all other officers
    3. To receive report from all officers
    4. To make recommendations to the General Assembly
    5. To act on all matters referred to it by the General Assembly
    6. To plan and co-ordinate at ASSOCIATION activities.
    BYE-LAW 10-3:
    VOTING
    At all meetings of the Board of Directors, each member of the Board shall have one vote.
    Voting shall be by show of hands. The Board of Directors shall meet as often as a majority may determine.

    BYE-LAW 10-4:
    QUORUM
    Four members at any meeting of the Board of Directors shall constitute a quorum

    BYE-LAW 10-5:
    BOARD JOINT MEETINGS
    A joint meeting of the newly elected Board of Directors and the out-going Board of Directors shall be held at a date agreed between the incoming president and the immediate past president but not later than two weeks from the convention.

    BYE-LAW 10-6:
    GENERAL JOINT MEETING BETWEEN NATIONAL, ZONAL AND STATE EXECUTIVES.
    This should be done twice in a year presided by National president at his secretariat (National secretariat).

    CHAPTER 11

    EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

    ARTICLE 11-1:

    COMPOSITION

    This committee shall comprise all the elected officers, appointed officers, the Directors and Chairmen.

    ARTICLE 11-2:
    The committee shall be responsible for resolving all emergencies of the ASSOCIATION and implement all decisions of the General Assembly and the Board of Directors.
    It shall approve and budget for the ensuring year and set the duties and composition of a long range planning committee and constitution review committee.
    It shall receive and consider all application for honorary titles and give advice for endorsement or rejection of same.

    BYE-LAW 11-3:

    VOTING

    At all meeting of the Executive Committee each member present shall have one vote.
    Voting shall be by show of hands. However, decisions of the Executive Committee shall be by consensus where otherwise the issue shall be referred to the Board of Directors.

    BYE-LAW 11-4:

    QUORUM AND MEETING

    The Executive Committee shall meet fortnightly. Five members shall form a quorum at any meeting of the committee.

    AREAS OF OPPORTUNITIES

    BYE-LAW 12-1:

    To facilitate the achievement of the purpose of the ASSOCIATION, its program of activities shall be divided into four areas of opportunities each under the direct supervision of the vice-presidents, who will communicate with ASSOCIATION officers responsible for program activities and bring information received to the attention of members.

    Individual Opportunities: To provide the opportunity for the individual member to realize his or her personal potentials through training program.

    Management Opportunities: To develop the managerial skills of the individual member by offering managerial training and actual experience as a leader at all levels of the organization.

    International and National Opportunities: To provide the opportunity for the individual member to contribute to the development of goodwill understanding and cooperation among all people.

    Community Opportunities: To develop the sensitivity of the individual member to societal problems through actual experience.

    Business Opportunities: To provide the opportunity for the individual members to contribute to the development and enhancement of the economic infrastructure, prosperity and well-being in all nations.

    BYE-LAW 12-2:

    DISCUSSION GROUPS

    Discussion group will be held at workshop for bringing before the members matters relating to the work of the areas of opportunities.

    DUES

    BYE-LAW 14-1:

    Members shall pay their yearly dues in three installments or as agreed by the House. Also they shall pay such dues and levies as imposed from time to time by the board of Directors upon ratification by the General Assembly.

    REGISTRATION FEES

    An agreed registration fee will be paid by new member and transferring members id card and membership form is #1000 while constitution fee will be based on annual conclusion because constitution is the heart of any organization once is not taken serious an organization will be useless.

    BYE-LAW 14-3:

    SUSPENSION

    Members whose payment of dues are more than three [3] months in arrears shall be suspended from membership of the ASSOCIATION and shall lose all JCI right until such dues have been paid.
    Any member who absences himself/herself from three consecutive meetings without apologies shall be suspended from membership of the ASSOCIATION.

    BYE-LAW 14-4:

    TERMINATION

    Members whose payment of dues is more than six [6] months in arrears shall cease to be a JCI except that member is on leave.
    Members who have fallen victim of Bye-law 14-3 for more than twice in a year without being on leave and members who have been involved in proven criminal cases or who have absented themselves for six consecutive meetings without apologies shall cease to be JCI.
